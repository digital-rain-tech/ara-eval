## Jurisdiction Context — Hong Kong (Grounded)

Apply the following regulatory requirements when evaluating this scenario.

### HKMA — Banking (GenAI Circular, Nov 2024 + BDAI Principles, 2019)

- **Fully autonomous AI decision-making is prohibited** in banking. Human oversight must be maintained to supervise, control, and intervene in AI decision-making processes.
- Customer-facing GenAI applications must allow customers to **opt out or request human intervention**.
- Institutions must implement **continuous monitoring** of GenAI output for quality, harmful, or misleading results.
- Three internal control mechanisms required: (i) AI ethics committee, (ii) designated oversight body, (iii) separate privacy risk management system.
- Board and senior management are **personally accountable** for AI application outcomes.
- AI applications must demonstrate **fairness** in customer outcomes — no discriminatory or biased results.
- **Transparency and disclosure** required: customers must know when AI is being used and understand how decisions affect them.
- GenAI Sandbox available for testing customer-facing applications under HKMA supervision.

### SFC — Securities & Investment (Circular 24EC55, Nov 2024)

- Applies to **all licensed corporations** using AI language models in regulated activities, whether internally developed, from group companies, or third-party.
- Four core principles: (i) Senior Management Oversight, (ii) AI Model Risk Management, (iii) Cybersecurity & Data Protection, (iv) Third-Party Risk Management.
- **High-risk applications** (investment advice, recommendations) require: rigorous output validation, human oversight to review AI-generated content, and disclosure of AI limitations to clients.
- Licensed corporations must **segregate roles** related to AI development and validation.
- Comprehensive testing and **detailed documentation** of AI models required.
- Due diligence required for third-party AI providers on governance and operational resilience.
- Effective immediately, though SFC takes a pragmatic approach to short-term compliance.

### PCPD — Data Protection (AI Framework, Jun 2024)

- Applies to all organisations that **procure, implement, and use** AI systems involving personal data (not just developers).
- Adopts a **risk-based approach** aligned with the Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance (PDPO).
- Four required areas: (i) AI strategy and governance, (ii) risk assessment and human oversight, (iii) AI model customisation/implementation/management, (iv) stakeholder communication and engagement.
- **Not legally binding** but represents PCPD's interpretation of PDPO compliance for AI — non-compliance may be considered in enforcement actions.

### PIPL — Cross-Border Data (Mainland China)

- Personal data transfer from mainland to Hong Kong requires one of: security assessment, security certification, or standard contractual clauses under PIPL.
- **GBA Standard Contracts** (GBA SCCs) available for transfers between Guangdong cities and Hong Kong SAR — a simplified mechanism for Greater Bay Area operations.
- March 2024 relaxation: CAC reduced cases requiring formal mechanisms, but **sensitive personal data** and **large-volume transfers** still require security assessment.
- January 2026: new Certification Measures for Cross-Border Transfer take effect, adding a certification pathway.

### CAC — Algorithm Registration

- AI services that use algorithms to **recommend content, make decisions, or generate synthetic content** for mainland-facing users must register with the CAC.
- Registration requires disclosing algorithm type, purpose, and self-assessment report.
- Applies to Hong Kong entities serving mainland Chinese users.
