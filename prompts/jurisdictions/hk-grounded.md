## Jurisdiction Context — Hong Kong (Grounded)

Apply the following regulatory requirements when evaluating this scenario.

### HKMA — Banking (GenAI Circular, Nov 2024 + BDAI Principles, 2019)

- **Fully autonomous AI decision-making is prohibited** in banking. Human oversight must be maintained to supervise, control, and intervene in AI decision-making processes. [^1][^2]
- Customer-facing GenAI applications must allow customers to **opt out or request human intervention**. [^1]
- Institutions must implement **continuous monitoring** of GenAI output for quality, harmful, or misleading results. [^1]
- Three internal control mechanisms required: (i) AI ethics committee, (ii) designated oversight body, (iii) separate privacy risk management system. [^2]
- Board and senior management are **personally accountable** for AI application outcomes. [^2]
- AI applications must demonstrate **fairness** in customer outcomes — no discriminatory or biased results. [^1][^2]
- **Transparency and disclosure** required: customers must know when AI is being used and understand how decisions affect them. [^1]
- GenAI Sandbox available for testing customer-facing applications under HKMA supervision. [^1]

### SFC — Securities & Investment (Circular 24EC55, Nov 2024)

- Applies to **all licensed corporations** using AI language models in regulated activities, whether internally developed, from group companies, or third-party. [^3]
- Four core principles: (i) Senior Management Oversight, (ii) AI Model Risk Management, (iii) Cybersecurity & Data Protection, (iv) Third-Party Risk Management. [^3]
- **High-risk applications** (investment advice, recommendations) require: rigorous output validation, human oversight to review AI-generated content, and disclosure of AI limitations to clients. [^3]
- Licensed corporations must **segregate roles** related to AI development and validation. [^3]
- Comprehensive testing and **detailed documentation** of AI models required. [^3]
- Due diligence required for third-party AI providers on governance and operational resilience. [^3]
- Effective immediately, though SFC takes a pragmatic approach to short-term compliance. [^3]

### PCPD — Data Protection (AI Framework, Jun 2024)

- Applies to all organisations that **procure, implement, and use** AI systems involving personal data (not just developers). [^4]
- Adopts a **risk-based approach** aligned with the Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance (PDPO). [^4]
- Four required areas: (i) AI strategy and governance, (ii) risk assessment and human oversight, (iii) AI model customisation/implementation/management, (iv) stakeholder communication and engagement. [^4]
- **Not legally binding** but represents PCPD's interpretation of PDPO compliance for AI — non-compliance may be considered in enforcement actions. [^4]

### PIPL — Cross-Border Data (Mainland China)

- Personal data transfer from mainland to Hong Kong requires one of: security assessment, security certification, or standard contractual clauses under PIPL Articles 38-43. [^5]
- **GBA Standard Contracts** (GBA SCCs) available for transfers between Guangdong cities and Hong Kong SAR — a simplified mechanism for Greater Bay Area operations. [^6]
- March 2024 relaxation: CAC reduced cases requiring formal mechanisms, but **sensitive personal data** and **large-volume transfers** still require security assessment. [^5]
- January 2026: new Certification Measures for Cross-Border Transfer take effect, adding a certification pathway. [^5]

### CAC — Algorithm Registration

- AI services that use algorithms to **recommend content, make decisions, or generate synthetic content** for mainland-facing users must register with the CAC. [^7]
- Registration requires disclosing algorithm type, purpose, and self-assessment report. [^7]
- Applies to Hong Kong entities serving mainland Chinese users. [^7]
