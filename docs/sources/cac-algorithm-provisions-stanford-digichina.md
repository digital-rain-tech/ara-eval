## Overview

The "Internet Information Service Algorithmic Recommendation Management Provisions" were jointly issued by the Cyberspace Administration of China (CAC), the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT), the Ministry of Public Security, and the State Administration for Market Regulation, effective March 1, 2022.

## Articles 24-26: Algorithm Filing Requirements

**Article 24** requires algorithmic recommendation service providers with "public opinion properties or social mobilization capabilities" to submit filings within 10 working days of commencing service, including:
- Provider's name
- Form of service
- Domain of application
- Algorithm type
- Algorithm self-assessment report
- Content intended to be publicized

Providers must also update filings within 10 working days when information changes and submit cancellation documents within 20 working days of ceasing operations.

**Article 25** establishes that national and provincial cybersecurity departments must grant filing status within 30 working days of receiving complete materials and issue "a filing number and publish the matter."

**Article 26** mandates that providers "indicate their filing number in a clear position on their website, application program, etc., used for providing external services, and provide a link to the published information."

## Key Regulatory Oversight

**Article 23** authorizes authorities to implement "graded and categorized management" based on factors including content type, user scale, data sensitivity, and user behavior interference levels.

**Articles 27-28** require security assessments for services with opinion-shaping capacity, with authorities providing "suggestions to correct discovered problems and provide a time limit for rectification."
