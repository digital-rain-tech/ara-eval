# UnitedHealth Class Action Lawsuit Over nH Predict Algorithm

- **Source:** STAT News
- **Date:** 14 November 2023
- **URL:** https://www.statnews.com/2023/11/14/unitedhealth-class-action-lawsuit-algorithm-medicare-advantage/
- **Archived:** 2026-03-11

---

## Overview

A class action lawsuit was filed against UnitedHealth Group and subsidiary NaviHealth on November 14, 2023, alleging systematic use of an algorithm called "nH Predict" to illegally deny rehabilitation care to seriously ill Medicare Advantage patients.

## The Algorithm

The nH Predict tool predicts patient length of stay in nursing facilities. UnitedHealth pressured medical staff to follow algorithmic recommendations, setting performance targets requiring case managers to keep patient rehab stays within 1% of the algorithm's projections.

## Error Rate and Appeal Data

The lawsuit alleges the algorithm has approximately a 90% error rate, calculated by measuring how many payment denials were reversed through internal appeals or administrative law judge rulings. The company reportedly knew only 0.2% of denied patients would file appeals.

## Named Plaintiffs and Cases

**Gene Lokken (91, Wisconsin):**
After fracturing his leg and ankle in May 2022, Lokken received minimal physical therapy. UnitedHealth approved only 19 days of therapy despite doctor recommendations. His family paid approximately $150,000 out-of-pocket for continued care before his death in July 2023.

**Dale Tetzloff (74, Wisconsin):**
Following a stroke in October 2022, Tetzloff's doctor recommended long-term nursing home care. The companies terminated coverage after 20 days. His family spent $70,000 for out-of-pocket care. Tetzloff died in assisted living in October 2023.

## Legal Claims

The complaint alleges:
- Breach of contract
- Breach of good faith and fair dealing
- Unjust enrichment
- Insurance law violations across multiple states

The lawsuit states the companies operated "a fraudulent scheme" denying promised benefits while collecting premiums.

## Company Response

UnitedHealth stated the tool serves as "a guide" for providers and families rather than determining coverage. The company emphasized that "coverage decisions are based on CMS coverage criteria" and vowed to "defend ourselves vigorously."
