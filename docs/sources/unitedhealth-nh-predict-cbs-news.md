# UnitedHealth Sued Over AI System Denying Elderly Patient Coverage

- **Source:** CBS News
- **Date:** 14 November 2023
- **URL:** https://www.cbsnews.com/news/unitedhealth-lawsuit-ai-deny-claims-medicare-advantage-health-insurance-denials/
- **Archived:** 2026-03-11

---

## The Lawsuit

Families of two deceased UnitedHealth beneficiaries filed suit in federal court in Minnesota, alleging the insurer knowingly deployed a defective artificial intelligence system to deny elderly patients medically necessary care covered under Medicare Advantage plans.

## The Algorithm

The problematic system, called "nH Predict" and developed by NaviHealth, reportedly had a 90% error rate. According to the complaint, the algorithm "override[d] determinations made by the patients' physicians" about medical necessity, with the AI model "disagreeing with their real live doctors' determinations."

## Core Allegations

The plaintiffs contend UnitedHealth systematically used the flawed AI to deny claims as a financial strategy, knowing that only about 0.2% of policyholders appeal denials. They argue most elderly beneficiaries either "pay out-of-pocket costs or forgo the remainder of their prescribed post-acute care."

## Company Response

NaviHealth spokesperson Aaron Albright stated the tool serves merely as "a guide to help inform providers" about potential patient needs, not to make coverage decisions. He claimed coverage determinations rely on "CMS coverage criteria" and the lawsuit "has no merit."

## Industry Context

Medical organizations like the American Medical Association have cautioned that "AI is not a silver bullet," urging insurers to require human review before denying coverage.
