# SFC Circular 24EC55: Use of Generative AI Language Models by Licensed Corporations

## Scope

Applies to all licensed corporations (LCs) offering services or functionality provided by AI language models (AI LMs) or AI LM-based third-party products in relation to regulated activities. Applicable regardless of whether the AI LM is developed by the LC itself, a group company, an external Third Party Provider, or comes from open source.

Requirements may be implemented in a **risk-based manner**, commensurate with materiality of impact and level of risk of the specific use case.

## Core Principles

### Core Principle 1: Senior Management Responsibilities
- Senior management must ensure throughout the **full lifecycle** of an AI LM:
  - (a) Adequate resources and procedures
  - (b) Adequate senior management oversight and governance by **suitably qualified and experienced individuals**
- Must be aware of risks and limitations of the AI LM and input data
- Must ensure AI LM is **fit for purpose** and appropriate for the specific use case

## Key Requirements

- **Risk-based implementation** — proportionate to materiality and risk level
- **Full lifecycle governance** — from development through deployment to decommissioning
- **Senior management accountability** — suitably qualified oversight
- **Fitness for purpose** — AI LM must be appropriate for the specific use case given its risks and limitations
- **Immediate effect** — LCs must critically review existing policies, procedures and internal controls
- SFC will take a **pragmatic approach** in assessing compliance, recognising some LCs need time to update
