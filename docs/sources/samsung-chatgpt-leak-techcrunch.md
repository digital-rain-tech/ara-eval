# Samsung Bans Generative AI Tools After Internal Data Leak

- **Source:** TechCrunch (Kate Park)
- **Date:** 2 May 2023
- **URL:** https://techcrunch.com/2023/05/02/samsung-bans-use-of-generative-ai-tools-like-chatgpt-after-april-internal-data-leak/
- **Archived:** 2026-03-11

---

## The Incident

Samsung experienced an accidental data breach in April 2023 when employees uploaded sensitive internal information to ChatGPT. Three separate incidents occurred within a short period:

- An engineer pasted proprietary source code into ChatGPT to check for errors
- Another employee submitted semiconductor equipment defect data
- A third employee uploaded a recording of an internal meeting for transcription/summarization

All three cases involved confidential corporate data being sent to OpenAI's servers, where it could potentially be used as training data.

## Samsung's Response

In response to the breaches, Samsung implemented a temporary company-wide ban on generative AI tool usage, covering:

- Company-owned devices (computers, tablets, smartphones)
- Non-company devices accessing internal networks

The restriction was announced in early May 2023, roughly one month after the initial data exposure. Samsung determined that prohibiting access to these AI services was necessary to protect confidential information.

## Key Takeaway

The incident highlights a critical vulnerability many organizations face: employees using public AI platforms for work tasks without adequate data protection safeguards. Samsung's precautionary measure reflects broader corporate concerns about intellectual property exposure when using third-party generative AI applications.
