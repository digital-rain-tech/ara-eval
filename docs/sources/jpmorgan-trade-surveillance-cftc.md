# CFTC Orders J.P. Morgan to Pay $200 Million for Trade Surveillance Failures

- **Source:** CFTC Press Release 8914-24
- **Date:** 14 March 2024
- **URL:** https://www.cftc.gov/PressRoom/PressReleases/8914-24
- **Archived:** 2026-03-11

---

## Penalty and Charges

The CFTC ordered J.P. Morgan Securities LLC to pay $200 million in civil penalties for failing to properly supervise its operations. The agency found that the firm did not adequately monitor billions of client orders on a U.S. designated contract market. The penalty would be reduced by $100 million offset against settlements with banking regulators (the OCC and Federal Reserve).

## Timeline and Scope of Violations

From 2014 through 2021, J.P. Morgan's surveillance systems failed to capture and monitor billions of order messages. The company discovered these gaps in 2021 while onboarding a new trading exchange. According to the order, "billions of order messages" went unsurveilled during this seven-year period, primarily involving sponsored access trading activity for three algorithmic trading firms. J.P. Morgan reported full remediation by 2023.

## Root Cause

The firm misconfigured data feeds to its surveillance tools, assuming that direct-from-exchange data sources were sufficiently reliable and required no verification. Management failed to apply their existing quarterly reconciliation process to these direct data feeds, incorrectly treating them as trustworthy "golden sources."

## Settlement Terms

J.P. Morgan admitted to specific factual violations, agreed to cease violations, and must comply with remedial conditions including the appointment of a consultant to oversee corrective measures.
