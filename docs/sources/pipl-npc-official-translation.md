# Personal Information Protection Law of the People's Republic of China (PIPL)

- **Source:** National People's Congress (official English translation)
- **Date:** Adopted 20 August 2021, effective 1 November 2021
- **URL:** http://en.npc.gov.cn.cdurl.cn/2021-12/29/c_694559.htm
- **Archived:** 2026-03-11 (from manual download of HTML; page 1 of 3 — Articles 1–25)

## Chapter I: General Provisions

**Article 1** — Enacted to protect rights and interests on personal information, regulate processing activities, and promote reasonable use.

**Article 2** — Personal information of natural persons is protected by law.

**Article 3** — Applies to processing of personal information within PRC territory. Also applies extraterritorially when: (1) providing products/services to persons in PRC; (2) analyzing/evaluating behavior of persons in PRC; (3) other circumstances per law.

**Article 4** — "Personal information" = various information related to an identified or identifiable natural person recorded electronically or otherwise. Does not include anonymized information. Processing includes collection, storage, use, processing, transmission, provision, disclosure, and deletion.

**Article 5** — Processing must be lawful, necessary, justified, in good faith. No misguidance, fraud, or coercion.

**Article 6** — Processing must have explicit and reasonable purposes, be directly related to those purposes, and exert minimum impact on individual rights. Collection limited to minimum scope.

**Article 7** — Openness and transparency required. Rules must be disclosed, purposes/means/scope explicitly indicated.

**Article 8** — Quality of personal information must be guaranteed to avoid adverse impacts from inaccuracy.

**Article 9** — Processors must be responsible and take necessary security measures.

**Article 10** — No illegal collection, use, processing, transmission, trading, provision, or disclosure.

**Article 11** — State shall establish personal information protection system.

**Article 12** — State engages in international cooperation on personal information protection rules.

## Chapter II: Personal Information Processing Rules

### Section 1: General Rules

**Article 13** — Processing permitted when: (1) individual consent obtained; (2) necessary for contract performance; (3) necessary for statutory duties; (4) necessary for public health/emergency response; (5) reasonable processing for news/public interest; (6) processing of legally disclosed information; (7) other circumstances per law.

**Article 14** — Consent must be voluntary, explicit, and fully informed. New consent required for changed purposes/means.

**Article 15** — Individuals have right to withdraw consent. Withdrawal does not affect validity of prior processing.

**Article 16** — Processors may not refuse products/services solely because individual withholds or withdraws consent (unless processing is necessary for provision).

**Article 17** — Before processing, processor must inform individual of: (1) processor name and contact; (2) purposes, means, categories, and storage periods; (3) methods for exercising rights; (4) other matters per law.

**Article 18** — Notification not required when law requires confidentiality. In emergencies, notify after emergency is removed.

**Article 19** — Storage period = minimum time necessary for processing purpose.

**Article 20** — Joint processors must agree on respective rights and obligations. Joint and several liability for damages.

**Article 21** — Entrusted processing requires agreement on purposes, period, means, categories, protections. Entrusted party processes per agreement only; no sub-contracting without consent.

**Article 22** — Transfer due to merger/dissolution etc. requires informing individuals. Recipient continues processor obligations.

**Article 23** — Providing personal information to another processor requires separate consent. Recipient processes within agreed scope.

**Article 24** — Automated decision-making must ensure transparency, fairness, and impartiality. No unreasonable differential treatment. Individuals may request clarification and refuse decisions made solely through automated processing.

**Article 25** — Processors shall not disclose personal information without separate consent.

**Note:** This archive covers page 1 of 3 of the NPC translation (Articles 1–25). Chapter III (Articles 38–43 on cross-border transfers) is on pages 2–3 and was not captured in the manual download. The cross-border transfer provisions most relevant to ARA-Eval scenarios include:

- **Article 38** — Cross-border provision requires one of: (1) security assessment by CAC; (2) certification by professional institution; (3) standard contract per CAC rules; (4) other conditions per law/treaty.
- **Article 39** — Processors must ensure foreign recipients meet PRC protection standards.
- **Article 40** — Critical information infrastructure operators and large-volume processors must store personal information domestically; cross-border provision requires security assessment.
- **Article 43** — Reciprocal countermeasures against countries/regions that take discriminatory actions against PRC in personal information protection.
