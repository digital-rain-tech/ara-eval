# AIA Hong Kong Record Fine for Anti-Money Laundering Lapses

- **Source:** Insurance Journal
- **Date:** 2 August 2024
- **URL:** https://www.insurancejournal.com/news/international/2024/08/02/786736.htm
- **Archived:** 2026-03-11

---

Hong Kong's Insurance Authority imposed a record penalty of HK$23 million ($2.9 million) on AIA Group Ltd. for anti-money laundering deficiencies. This represents the largest fine since the regulator assumed oversight of insurers in 2017.

## Investigation Period

The on-site inspection examined business activities spanning March 2016 through October 2022, when regulators identified systemic compliance issues.

## AML Algorithm Failures

The Insurance Authority discovered "technical issues" with AIA's anti-money laundering system and its associated screening algorithm. Specifically, the system failed to properly identify certain customers classified as politically exposed persons during initial screening procedures. These lapses resulted in delays in verifying the source and legitimacy of customer wealth.

Additionally, AIA's Hong Kong unit did not promptly apply "enhanced due diligence in a timely manner" to clients flagged as high-risk by the system.

## Investigation Outcome

The regulator confirmed that no policies were inappropriately sold to customers who should have been rejected. AIA stated the company cooperated fully with inspections beginning in 2021 and characterized findings as "technical in nature" with no evidence of actual money laundering activities.

The company must now submit a report from an independent advisor documenting the effectiveness of remediation efforts.
