## Summary

(Article content extracted from manually saved HTML page)

Samsung Semiconductor engineers leaked confidential data to ChatGPT in at least three separate incidents within a span of roughly 20 days after the company lifted its ban on the AI tool in late March 2023.

## Key Incidents

1. **Source code leak:** An engineer pasted proprietary semiconductor source code into ChatGPT to check for bugs
2. **Equipment data leak:** Another engineer submitted sensitive data related to Samsung's semiconductor equipment and yield to ask ChatGPT to optimize the code
3. **Meeting transcript leak:** An employee recorded an internal meeting, converted it to text, and submitted the transcript to ChatGPT for meeting notes

## Samsung's Response

Samsung subsequently imposed restrictions on ChatGPT and other GenAI tools, limiting prompt lengths and warning employees about data risks. The company also began developing its own internal AI tool.

## Industry Impact

The incident became a widely-cited cautionary tale about GenAI data leakage risks in enterprise settings, prompting many financial institutions to restrict or ban the use of external GenAI tools.
