# Insurance Authority Fine Against AIA International Limited

- **Source:** Insurance Authority (Hong Kong) press release
- **Date:** 2 August 2024
- **URL:** https://www.ia.org.hk/en/infocenter/press_releases/20240802.html
- **Archived:** 2026-03-11

---

**Fine Amount:** HK$23 million

**Company:** AIA International Limited (Hong Kong branch)

## Violations Found

The Insurance Authority identified technical deficiencies in AIA's anti-money laundering system during an inspection covering March 2016 to October 2022. Specific issues included:

- The AML system failed to properly identify politically exposed persons, resulting in missed screening alerts
- Delays in establishing source of funds and obtaining senior management approval as mandated
- High-risk customers were not subjected to enhanced due diligence procedures in timely manner
- Inadequate monitoring processes for suspicious transactions
- Gaps in AML compliance and management oversight

The violations referenced sections 5(1), 10(1), 10(2), 15, 19(1), 19(3), and 23 of Schedule 2 to the AMLO.

## Remediation Requirements

AIA must "submit to the IA by a date and in a manner to be specified by the IA, a report prepared by an independent external advisor to validate the ongoing effectiveness of the remediation measures."

The Authority acknowledged AIA's "cooperative approach" and noted the company has already invested substantially in system upgrades and committed to independent validation of controls across group operations.
