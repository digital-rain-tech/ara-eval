## Summary

JPMorgan Chase was fined nearly $350 million in penalties by multiple regulators for widespread failures in trade surveillance and reporting over a seven-year period (2014–2021).

## Combined Penalties

The total fine package included settlements with:
- CFTC: $200 million (see `jpmorgan-trade-surveillance-cftc.md` for CFTC details)
- OCC and Federal Reserve: additional penalties
- Total: approximately $548 million across all regulators

## Root Cause

J.P. Morgan's surveillance systems failed to capture and monitor billions of order messages due to misconfigured data feeds. The firm assumed direct-from-exchange data sources were reliable and required no verification, incorrectly treating them as "golden sources."
